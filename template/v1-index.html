Welcome

As students and educators, we believe textbooks cost too much and offer too little. The staid process of creating the books results in outdated teaching materials and place an unfair economic burden on students. We believe it is time for disruption of this process. We envision the day when every student has an e-reader and will access learning materials online. In this rapidly approaching reality, the greatest limitation on educators and students will be access to high-quality content. Inspired by the success of projects like Wikipedia and the open-access software movement, we believe that collectively we can create our own instructional materials. As the first step, we have launched a global effort to create a prototype system and alpha-test concepts through the collaborative development of a textbook for advanced undergraduates studying cancer genetics.
